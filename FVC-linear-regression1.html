In the model we see Age, Height, Male, PM2.5, & Nitrate all have positive significant association. Age and height both are positive predictors, for every unit increase in height FVC increases by about 59.688 units; this is expected since older and larger people have larger lung capacity. Nitrate shows a significant positive association (~109.107) & so does PM2.5 (9.761), sometimes in environmental studies this is due to col-linearity (pollutants being highly correlated with each other) which can flip the sign of a coefficient. While all pollutants have a effect their p-values do not meet the 5% line. Male is binary and is associated with an average increase of 395.372 units in FVC compared to females, this holds all other variables constant.

Sulfate & Elemental Carbon has a different story. Sulfate has a significant negative impact, for every unit increased in sulfate levels decreases FVC by about 348.111 units (KEY FINDING). EC in this model doesn’t really have a statistically significant effect on FVC in this model.

So from my understanding Sulfate may be the strongest pollutant predictor here, with male (sex) in close following
